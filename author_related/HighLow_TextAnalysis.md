# LWC Domain Legend (Human-Readable)

Use this legend to keep `(mode, audience)` buckets consistent when labeling samples, planning prompts, or reporting validator output. Descriptions are plain language equivalents of the legacy symbolic formulas.

| Domain | Code | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Applications | LWC01 | Formal responses written for evaluative review; measured tone that anticipates judgment and foregrounds qualifications. |
| Blogs | LWC02 | Public-facing rhetorical essays or commentary meant for broad audiences with a personal point of view. |
| Conversations | LWC03 | Text-message or chat-style exchanges with interpersonal focus and light meta-commentary. |
| Emails | LWC04 | Professional correspondence with clear requests, status updates, and directional guidance. |
| Facebook | LWC05 | Personal social posts for friends/family that mix narrative transparency with social updates. |
| Movies | LWC06 | Cinematic narration with staged observation and performative framing. |
| Novels | LWC07 | Immersive, world-building fiction with detailed scene capture and ordered pacing. |
| NYT | LWC08 | Journalistic opinion or validation pieces with formal tone, evidence, and editorial framing. |
| Reddit | LWC09 | Ego-forward social commentary with positional framing, spotlight bias, and informal tone. |
| Short Story | LWC10 | Concise fiction that leans on detailed description and tight narrative scaffolding. |
| Stream of Consciousness | LWC11 | Filtered internal narration that drifts in time and perspective. |
| Speeches | LWC12 | Rhetorical speeches that balance accusation/appeal, meta-framing, and clear calls to action. |
| TAT | LWC13 | Reactional, self-relational writing that foregrounds appraisal and performative reflection. |
| Tweets | LWC14 | Public micro-posts optimized for brevity, stance, and rhetorical clarity. |
| Yelp | LWC15 | Critical reviews focused on social reward/punishment and evaluative scoring. |
